Who we are

NOBLE WINGS ACADEMY
- AIM HIGH...

NOBLE WINGS is a modern aviation center founded in 2014, dedicated for practical and theoretical pilot training.

Our headquarters is located in Adela Dankowska Airport in Leszno, capital of gliding aviation and one of the most popular and famous general aviation airports in Poland.

We have our own training bases in Wroclaw and Leszno, as well as a dedicated 0-ATL (A) training center in Mumbai. We focus on training candidates for airline or corporate pilots, although we also take into account people for whom flying is a springboard from the hardships of everyday life or a way for quick private or company transport.

We are Pilots, so we perfectly understand how important is to adjust the course of training to the individual needs of candidate and appropriate way of its implementation, while respecting the time and financial resources of the future pilot. We select the appropriate instructors, aircraft type and plan the pace of training so as to achieve its maximum efficiency.

On top of training offer we provide full scope of aircraft maintenance service, mainly for internal fleet and for clients, who purchased aircrafts from our sale department. Incidentally we provide the ad hoc maintenance works for other clients.

LEADERSHIP

The Architects of Aviation Excellence

Meet the leaders whose passion and experience laid the foundation for Noble Wings Academy.

Michał Skowroński

Head of Training

Michał began his aviation journey in 1993 as a glider pilot. In 2000 he graduated from the Polish National Civil Pilots School while simultaneously earning a Master of Science degree in Aviation Systems Engineering.

His airline career includes operations with Polish Air Medical Rescue, Scandinavian Air System Flying Academy, Blue Jet Poland and Polish Airlines. Throughout his career he accumulated over 13,000 flight hours, including more than 10,000 hours as Captain.

In 2014 he founded Noble Wings Aviation School in Poland and later established Noble Wings Technika. He continues to actively serve as Head of Training and Flight Instructor.

Tomasz Nawrocki

Accountable Manager

Tomasz combines aviation operations experience with aviation management expertise. He obtained all required pilot qualifications within one year and currently serves as Accountable Manager, Product Manager and Practical Training Instructor.

His background includes entrepreneurship, aviation education and instructor development. He is recognized for his resilience, technical knowledge and commitment to training quality.

Alongside management responsibilities, Tomasz continues to pursue his aviation career as a First Officer while actively supporting the growth of Noble Wings Academy.

Rasikal Tapase

World Sales Director

Rasikal Tapase is an aviation professional from India with a Bachelor's Degree in Business Administration and an FAA-issued Pilot License. He plays a key role in connecting aviation expertise with business development.

Before joining Noble Wings Academy, he served as Director for a respected flying club in the Maldives, leading student admissions and sales operations.

Since joining Noble Wings Academy Asia, Rasikal has successfully expanded operations across Asia and the Middle East, helping the academy achieve significant student growth while strengthening international aviation partnerships.

Technical Department

NOBLE WINGS TECHNIKA
- Aircraft Maintenance & Airworthiness.

We have our own maintenance facility and properly trained technical staff. Thanks to this, we are able to effectively care for the fleet of school planes without generating unnecessary downtime.

Our continuing airworthiness management department plans the maintenance of individual aircraft in advance, taking care of providing the required spare parts, tools and constantly verifying aircraft documentation in terms of possible needs.

In this way we ensured the highest possible level of safety for our training operations.

Our maintenance organization is approved by the Civil Aviation Authority, which is confirmed by the Combined Airworthiness Organization certificate number PL.CAO.0093.
